Racing Wheel — Overall Strength

Tune global force levels and safe working limits. This section is your quick‑access to overall feel and headroom.

Racing Wheel Overall Strength FFB Levels
UI Location
Racing Wheel page • section Overall Strength
What this section does
Sets MAIRA’s overall “feel” via Wheel Force and Strength, defines the ceiling with Max Force, and provides automatic helpers using Auto Margin plus Set/Clear.

Wheel Force

  • Default: 5
  • What it does: Sets the physical wheelbase force (Nm) MAIRA targets when generating FFB.
  • How it works: Used across algorithms to scale torques so 1.0 equals your selected wheel force.
  • Notes: Not sure what to set here? Here is a list of common wheel forces - Racing Wheel List

Strength

  • Default: 10%
  • What it does: Global output gain. Think of it as the master “feel” knob for car‑to‑car adjustments.
  • Tips: Map +/− buttons for quick tweaks; MAIRA provides separate plus/minus mappings.

Max Force

  • Default: 50 Nm
  • What it does: Sets the raw effective upper steering wheel torque (before scaling).
  • Notes: Has dedicated +/− mappings for on‑the‑fly changes.
Tip: Wheel force, strength, and max force are all related by this equation: maxForce = wheelForce strength

Auto Margin

  • Default: 0%
  • What it does: Margin that MAIRA uses when auto‑setting headroom. Positive values keep extra room to avoid clipping on spikes. Negative values make the wheel feel stronger at the expense of more clipping.
  • Controls: Set uses the Auto Max Force value to update the Strength and Max Force settings for you; Clear clears the Auto Max Force so you can re‑measure.
  • Tips: Map Set and Clear buttons so you can fire them while you are driving.
Pro tip: Bind Strength plus/minus to convenient wheel buttons for quick mid‑race adjustments, use Clear at the start of a lap, and use Set after a few corners to capture realistic peaks for your current combo.
