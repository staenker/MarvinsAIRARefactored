Racing Wheel

Overall Strength

Tune global force levels and safe working limits. This section is your quick‑access to overall feel and headroom.

Wheel Force

Sets the maximum physical wheelbase force (Nm).

Please this to the actual maximum force specified by the manufacturer.

Used across algorithms to scale torques so 1.0 equals your the maximum physical wheel force.

Not sure what to set here? Here is a list of common wheel forces - Racing Wheel List

Default: 5 Nm

Range: 2 → 50 Nm

Strength

Global output gain. Think of it as the master “feel” knob for car‑to‑car adjustments.

Map +/− buttons for quick tweaks; MAIRA provides separate plus/minus mappings.

Default: 10%

Range: 0 → 100%

Max Force

Sets the raw effective simulated steering wheel torque limit.

This is the maximum wheel force from the iRacing simulator that will not be clipped.

Has dedicated +/− mappings for on‑the‑fly changes.

Default: 50 Nm

Range: 0 → 300 Nm

Wheel force, strength, and max force are all related by this equation: maxForce = wheelForce strength

Auto Margin

Margin that MAIRA uses when auto‑setting headroom. Positive values keep extra room to avoid clipping on spikes. Negative values make the wheel feel stronger at the expense of more clipping.

Set uses the Auto Max Force value to update the Strength and Max Force settings for you; Clear clears the Auto Max Force so you can re‑measure.

Map Set and Clear buttons so you can fire them while you are driving.

Default: 0%

Range: -100 → 100%

Pro tip: Bind Strength plus/minus to convenient wheel buttons for quick mid‑race adjustments, use Clear at the start of a lap, and use Set after a few corners to capture realistic peaks for your current combo.
