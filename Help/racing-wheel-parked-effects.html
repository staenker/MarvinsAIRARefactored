Racing Wheel — Parked Effects

Gentle low‑speed behavior: fade overall strength down, add a touch of friction, and optionally keep the wheel centered while stopped.

Racing Wheel Parked Effects Low‑Speed Behavior
UI Location
Racing Wheel page • section Parked Effects
What this section does
As speed drops from 5 mph → 0, MAIRA cross‑fades from your normal racing feel to a parked profile: overall force is scaled toward a safer value, base friction is swapped for parked friction, and (optionally) a centering force is applied.

Strength

  • Default: 10%
  • Range: 0–100%
  • OFF: 100% (no reduction when fully parked)
  • What it does: Scales the final output torque while stopped. At 10%, parked torque is one‑tenth of your normal strength at 0 mph, blending up to full strength by ~5 mph.

Friction

  • Default: OFF (0%)
  • Range: 0–100%
  • OFF: 0% (no added parked friction)
  • What it does: Adds a small friction torque only when stopped, which helps calm micro‑oscillation or wheel wobble in the pits. As you roll away, it blends back to your normal racing friction.

Center wheel while parked

  • Default: On
  • What it does: Applies the standard wheel‑centering torque when parked (uses your global Centering Strength). It’s blended with the Center wheel while racing setting based on speed.
Pro tip: If your wheel oscillates in the pits, increae Friction and enable Center wheel while parked. If the wheel feels too heavy while stopped, lower Strength.
