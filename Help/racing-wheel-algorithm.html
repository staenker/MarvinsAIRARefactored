Racing Wheel

Algorithm

Choose the force‑feedback shaping algorithm and tune its core parameters.

Algorithm

Native 60 Hz

Minimal shaping; normalizes the game’s 60 Hz torque straight to your Max Force. Clean, classic feel.

Native 360 Hz

Higher‑rate pass‑through normalized to Max Force. Reduces “stepping.”

Detail Booster

Expands high‑frequency detail. Great for curb/road texture without big changes to weight.

Delta Limiter

Caps how fast torque can change (rate‑of‑change limiter) to tame spikes while preserving the base feel.

Detail Booster (on 60 Hz)

Same as Detail Booster, but done by expanding the 360 Hz details and applying that on top of the 60 Hz base for lower latency.

Delta Limiter (on 60 Hz)

Same as Delta Limiter, but done by limiting the 360 Hz details and applying that on top of the 60 Hz base for lower latency.

Slew + Total Compression

Two‑stage compressor: one engages on rapid changes (slew), the other on overall magnitude (total). Smooths big hits without crushing detail.

Multi‑Adjustment Toolkit

A toolbox chain you can tune: add detail, smooth the output, limit slew, and compress peaks — all in one place.

What each setting does

Detail Booster

Detail Boost — How much high‑frequency detail to blend in. Higher = crisper road feel.

Detail Boost Bias — Balance between the steady base vs. added detail. Higher bias = more weight on the base, gentler detail injection.

Delta Limiter

Delta Limit — Caps the rate of change of torque (Nm/sec). Stops sudden jumps; smoother transitions.

Delta Limiter Bias — Blend between limited vs. original signal. Higher bias = retains more of the original; lower = stronger limiting feel.

Slew + Total Compression

Slew Threshold — When rapid‑change compression kicks in. Lower = engages sooner on spikes.

Slew Compression Rate — How strongly those rapid changes are compressed after the threshold.

Total Threshold — Overall torque level where peak compression begins.

Total Compression Rate — Strength of that overall (peak) compression once engaged.

Multi‑Adjustment Toolkit

Detail Gain — Adds crispness by mixing in a controlled amount of high‑frequency detail.

Output Smoothing — Gently smooths the running torque; reduces chatter and rough edges.

Slew Rate Reduction — Limits how fast torque can rise/fall to prevent shocky hits.

Torque Compression — Compresses big peaks so strong forces stay controllable.

Soft Clipping

Soft clipping is a gentle saturation that rounds off the very top of the signal instead of chopping it flat. In MAIRA it’s applied after the algorithm, so it works with every algorithm. When enabled, it:

    Softens harsh peaks that can feel spiky or fatiguing.

    Preserves most mid‑range detail (unlike a hard limiter).

    Helps avoid sudden jolts if the signal briefly exceeds your headroom.

You’ll find it in the same section as the algorithm settings. Toggle it on if peaks feel too “square,” or off if you want the full raw punch.
