Steering Effects

Thresholds

Configure the detection bands for Understeer, Oversteer, and Seat of Pants (SOP). Each uses a Minimum and Maximum threshold to map raw telemetry into a 0→1 effect signal.

Understeer

Understeer is the shortfall in yaw‑rate vs. expected for the current steering input.

Thresholds are shown in °/s (degrees per second), derived from the calibration graph.

This effect drives the Wheel/Pedal Vibration, Wheel Constant Force, and the related sound on the Sounds page.

Use the +/− button mappings to tune the effect in real-time.

Defaults: Minimum 0.05, Maximum 0.15.

Oversteer

Oversteer is excess yaw‑rate vs. expected (the car rotates more than the steering angle predicts).

Thresholds shown in °/s (degrees per second), derived from the calibration graph.

This effect drives the same Wheel/Pedal and related sound effects as Understeer.

Use the +/− button mappings to tune the effect in real-time.

Defaults: Minimum 0.00, Maximum 0.50.

Seat of Pants (SOP)

Lateral “seat” cue derived from your chosen algorithm (see below).

Use the +/− button mappings to tune the effect in real-time.

Units & defaults: Minimum 0.00, Maximum 3.00. Units depend on algorithm:

Lateral Acceleration (Ay) → g

Lateral Velocity (Vy) → m/s

Ratio of Velocities (Vy/Vx) → unitless ratio

SOP Algorithm

Choose how the SOP signal is computed:

Lateral Acceleration (Ay) — based on lateral g‑force; clearer at steady speeds.

Lateral Velocity (Vy) — sideways velocity; emphasizes rapid lateral movements.

Ratio of Velocities (Vy/Vx) (default) — normalizes lateral motion by forward speed for a speed‑independent feel.

Tuning

Experience
What to do
Cues trigger too late
Lower the minimum threshold
Cues trigger too often
Raise the minimum threshold
Effect ramps up too slowly (or never reaches full strength)
Lower the maximum threshold
Effect ramps up too fast or is too abrupt
Raise the maximum threshold
Pro tip: If you want the effect to turn on/off instead of ramping up and down, you can set minimum = maximum threshold.
