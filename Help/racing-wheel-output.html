What this section does
Shapes the final torque via Minimum, Maximum, and Curve. It’s the last stop before force reaches your wheel.

Minimum

  • Default: 0% (OFF)
  • Range: 0%–10%
  • What it does: Raises very small torques up to a floor (and mirrors for negative). This helps overcome static friction and makes subtle forces around center more noticeable.
  • Display: OFF when at 0%
Pro tip: Only use this for gear or belt driven wheels; leave this off for direct drive wheels.

Maximum

  • Default: 100% (OFF)
  • Range: 20%–100%
  • What it does: Caps the absolute torque magnitude symmetrically. Lower this to keep peaks comfortable on powerful bases.
  • Display: OFF when at 100%

Curve

  • Default: OFF (linear)
  • What it does: Shapes how torque grows with input. Increasing the curve applies a non-linear response: more or less sensitivity to mid‑range cues.
  • Order of operations: Applied before soft clipping and after the algorithm.
  • Display: OFF when set to 0%
