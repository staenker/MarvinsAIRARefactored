Steering Effects

Calibration File

Select a calibration file for your current car to enable Steering Effects and view the calibration graph that those effects rely on.

Calibration file dropdown

MAIRA lists CSV files found in your Calibration folder that match the current car. Only files for the car you’re driving are shown.

When you pick a file, MAIRA immediately loads it and updates the graph and any dependent settings.

Choose [Steering Effects Disabled] (the first option) to disable steering effects. This clears the selection and MAIRA treats Steering Effects as disabled.

Where files are stored

Calibration CSVs are saved under your Calibration folder. A typical filename is <Car Screen Name> - <Car Setup Name>.csv. The CSV header is Steering Wheel Angle,Yaw Rate.

About the calibration graph

Your recorded calibration data is rendered as a continuous blue line across steering angles, derived from the CSV values.

Understeer and Oversteer thresholds are displayed as yellow (minimum) and red (maximum) lines. As you adjust thresholds, you can see these lines moving on the graph.

Axes

X‑axis = Steering Wheel Angle (degrees).

Y‑axis = Yaw Rate (degrees/second).
