Steering Effects — Wheel Constant Force Effect

Adds a directional constant torque to the wheel based on Understeer, Oversteer, or Seat of Pants signals.

Steering Effects Wheel Constant Force
UI Location
Steering Effects page • sections Understeer — Wheel Constant Force Effect, Oversteer — Wheel Constant Force Effect, and Seat of Pants — Wheel Constant Force Effect
What it does
Applies an extra torque that either reduces or increases the current FFB. Select Direction: None to disable this effect. The amount follows the detection signal (0→1), shaped by Curve and scaled by Strength.

Settings

  • Direction — None, Decrease Force, or Increase Force. Choosing None disables the effect.
    Defaults: Understeer None, Oversteer Increase Force, Seat of Pants None.
    Decrease Force blends output toward 0; Increase Force pushes in the current torque direction.
  • Strength — scale of the added/removed torque at full signal.
    Range: 0 → 100%. Defaults: Understeer 10%, Oversteer 50%, Seat of Pants 50%.
  • Curve — shapes how quickly the force builds with the signal.
    Range: −100 → +100%. Default: 0% (linear).

Supports +/− button mappings and Context Switches for per‑profile/car/track tuning.

Pro tip: Try Decrease Force for Understeer to “lighten” the wheel as grip is lost, and Increase Force for Oversteer to nudge counter‑steer reactions.
