Steering Effects — Wheel Vibration Effect

Generate patterned vibration on the wheel (selecting Vibration Pattern: None disables this effect) when Understeer, Oversteer, or Seat of Pants signals are active.

What it does
Applies a repeating vibration waveform to the wheel when the corresponding detection signal is above zero. Choose Vibration Pattern: None to disable this effect. Intensity follows the signal (0→1), then passes through a Curve and the chosen Vibration Pattern.

Settings

  • Vibration Pattern — waveform shape. Options: None, Sine Wave, Square Wave, Triangle Wave, Sawtooth Wave (In), Sawtooth Wave (Out).
    Defaults: Understeer Sine Wave, Oversteer None, Seat of Pants None.
  • Strength — how strong the vibration can get at full signal.
    Range: 0 → 30% (% in the UI). Default: 10% for all three.
  • Minimum Frequency — frequency used as the signal just exceeds the threshold.
    Range: 0 → 50 Hz. Default: 15 Hz.
  • Maximum Frequency — frequency used when the signal reaches 100%.
    Range: 0 → 50 Hz. Default: 50 Hz.
  • Curve — shapes how quickly the effect ramps with the signal.
    Range: −100 → +100%. Default: 25%. Negative values front‑load the response (more feel near threshold); positive values delay it until higher signal.

All controls support +/− button mappings and Context Switches so you can tune per profile, car, or track.

How frequency is chosen

Only Strength is interpolated with the signal. Frequency stays at Minimum Frequency until the signal reaches 100%, then switches to Maximum Frequency instantly. The Curve shapes how Strength grows, not the frequency.
