Steering Device

Choose which wheelbase MAIRA uses for force feedback and button input.

Racing Wheel FFB Output Device Selection
UI Location
Racing Wheel page • Dropdown labeled Steering Device
Default
Not selected (no device chosen / empty GUID)
What it does
Tells MAIRA which force-feedback (FFB) device to control. When selected, MAIRA routes FFB output and reads button inputs from this device.

How it works

  • MAIRA scans attached DirectInput devices and lists FFB-capable hardware in the dropdown.
  • When you pick a device, MAIRA saves that choice and immediately binds FFB output to it.
  • On future launches, MAIRA tries to re-attach to the saved device automatically.
  • If the device isn’t connected, you’ll see a gray entry like Device not found [GUID] until you reconnect or pick another.

When to change it

  • You have multiple wheelbases and swap between them.
  • Windows re-enumerated the device (e.g., different USB port, driver re-install).
  • First-time setup after installing MAIRA.

Tips & troubleshooting

  • Power on the wheelbase before opening MAIRA so it’s discoverable.
  • If you hot-plug devices, use the app’s refresh/reinitialize action to rescan and rebind quickly.
  • Selecting the wrong device is harmless—FFB simply won’t output until the correct one is chosen.
Heads-up: If your previously selected device is offline, the selection is preserved. Reconnect the device and re-open MAIRA (or refresh) to re-bind automatically.

Power-user details

MAIRA stores a Windows GUID for the device rather than just its label. This avoids ambiguity when two devices share a name.
