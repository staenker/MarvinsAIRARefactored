Racing Wheel — Wheel LFE

Route iRacing’s low‑frequency effects (LFE) into your wheel as gentle vibration — great for immersion, wheel‑slip cues, kerbs, and custom haptics.

Racing Wheel Wheel LFE Audio → FFB
UI Location
Racing Wheel page • section Wheel LFE
What this section does
MAIRA captures the LFE audio signal (via a virtual audio cable) and mixes it into your wheel’s force‑feedback. This adds tactile “rumble” information to the wheel without touching your base FFB algorithm.

Requirements

  • VB‑CABLE (free virtual audio device). Download and install it, then reboot.
  • iRacing LFE enabled and set to output to “CABLE Input (VB‑Audio Virtual Cable)”.

Setup checklist

  1. Install VB‑CABLE — Get it from VB‑AUDIO, run the x64 installer, and reboot. The installer can be found here - VB-AUDIO Software
  2. (Optional) Keep your bass shaker working — In Windows sound settings → Recording → select CABLE Output → Properties → Listen tab → enable Listen to this device and choose the playback device your shaker uses. This pass‑through lets the shaker and the wheel both receive LFE.
  3. Configure iRacing — Options → Misc → check Enable LFE, set the device to CABLE Input (VB‑Audio Virtual Cable). Adjust LFE sliders to taste (wheel slip is especially useful).
  4. Configure MAIRA — Racing Wheel Wheel LFE:
    • LFE Recording Device: Select CABLE Output (VB‑Audio Virtual Cable). Choose [Disabled] to turn Wheel LFE off.
    • Strength: Sets how much LFE is mixed into your wheel. Default is 5%. Range 0–100%.

How it works (under the hood)

  • MAIRA captures mono audio at 8 kHz / 16‑bit, averages to ~500 Hz frames, and mixes the resulting magnitude into your FFB.
  • Typical end‑to‑end latency is about 16–33 ms (≈ 1/60 to 1/30 s).
  • The LFE mix happens after the algorithm output and before soft‑lock/other effects.

Driving tips

  • Start around 5–10% and increase slowly. You want a subtle texture, not a second FFB layer.
  • Bind Strength +/− to wheel buttons so you can trim the effect live.
  • Combine with the Wheel Slip LFE slider in iRacing for clearer “on the limit” feel.
  • Fun extras: route SimHub effects into VB‑CABLE to create custom wheel vibrations, or even select a microphone/webcam to test with your voice.

Troubleshooting

  • No movement: Verify iRacing Enable LFE is on and the device is set to CABLE Input; in MAIRA, choose CABLE Output as the recording device.
  • Too strong/weak: Adjust Strength in MAIRA and LFE sliders in iRacing. If you use iRacing’s “-10 dB cut”, you may need a higher MAIRA strength.
Pro tip: Keep Wheel LFE subtle. It’s most effective as fine “texture” layered under your main FFB — especially for slip/kerbs — without masking steering weight.
